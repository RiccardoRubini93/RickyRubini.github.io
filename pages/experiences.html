Bachelor Degree in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Genoa

The quest starts at the end of summer 2011 at the University of Genoa where I started my adventure in the faculty of Mechanical Engineering. Three years and several exams later I took my bachelor degree in Mechanical engineering. This has been a quite important period for my personal and cultural growth. During my bachelor I had the opportunity to roam in quite broad variety of fields ranging from general mathematics and physics to information science and more specific topics concerning mechanical engineering. At the end of the first three years I (more or less) realized what I would like to be when I grow up, so I took my Bachelor Degree with a thesis about the evaluation of the capabilities of a CFD Open Source software (OpenFOAM) on a mass and heat transfer industrial problem. Master Degree Aeronautical Engineering at the University of Genoa

During the bachelor I had the opportunity to roam between different topics concerning the field of the Mechanical Engineering. This allowed me to choose for the master the field I liked the most. For this reason I undertake my Master Degree in Aeronautical Engineering. The main topic of my master has been Fluid Dynamics in all his flavors, focusing my interest on the numerical techniques such as Finite Difference, Finite Volume and Finite Elements. Moreover during the two years of Master Degree I star to scratch the surface of more advanced topics concerning the Aeronautical Engineering such as Compressible and Incompressible Aerodynamics and fundamentals notions of Aeroacoustic and Combustions. Finally I took my master degree in September 2016 with a Thesis concerning the development of a simplified model for the analysis of axial turbomachinery.

Main Field of Study

  • Mathematical and Numerical methods for the Engineering Science
  • Incompressible and Compressible Aerodynamic
  • Advanced thermodynamic and Heat Transfer
  • Transition to Turbulence and Turbulence Modeling
  • Aeroacoustic and Combustion

Research Master Programme von Karman Institute

After taking my degree in September 2016 I realized that the time to go abroad and experience new things and level up my knowledge has come. So I chose to apply for the nine months research master program at von Karman Institute. When I received a positive response I booked my flight and packed up my stuff. During the master I have had (am having) the opportunity to deepen my knowledge in many aspects of theoretical and experimental fluid dynamics thanks to the lectures and the laboratories hold by the Institute. However in my opinion the most interesting part of the master is the personal project you have to follow and develop during the nine months. Following my interest I chose a numerical project dealing with the study of bypass transition on a lifting profile. The main tool I have exploited are the RANS transition models.Here you have the Abstract of my project. At the end the results where quite satisfactory especially for one of the two model taken into consideration, in particular the one based on the concept of laminar kinetic energy.
